The exhibition begins with the word “Eolreongttungttang” (haphazardly).
Although the word usually implies an impromptu or seemingly insincere attitude,
within the inevitable imperfection of “Eolreongttungttang” that we face as students and amateurs,
there lies an opportunity to encounter our own individuality.

This exhibition embraces such “Eolreongttungttangness” as part of the work,
seeking to reveal the uncertainty and fluidity within the process.
Both participating teams focus on movement within the process,
starting from a clumsy and unstable state and expanding the theme in their own ways.

This exhibition begins with the word “Eolleongttungttang” (roughly meaning slapdash).
Although it generally refers to an impromptu or seemingly insincere attitude,
within the inevitable slapdash imperfection we experience as students and amateurs,
lies an opportunity to face our individuality.

This exhibition embraces such “slapdashness” as part of the work,
aiming to reveal uncertainty and fluidity within the process.
Both participating teams focus on movements within the process,
starting from clumsiness and instability to expand the theme in their own ways.

Team 1 starts from the concept of the “Coriolis force,” exploring the complexity of family relationships
and the flow of emotions within them. Team 2 explores the gap between social perspectives
and personal intimacy through a fake documentary about the daily life of a politically mismatched couple.

Though their approaches and contents differ, both teams share an attitude of attempting, composing,
and deciding from an unsettled state. This reflects the incomplete character, spontaneity,
and cautious experimentation implied by the word “slapdash.”

This exhibition does not present a clear theme or a dazzling conclusion.
Instead, it presents the creators’ inconsistent perspectives, emotions, and attempts as they are,
allowing the audience to encounter the imperfect questions and the raw attitude itself.

We think of ourselves as complete individuals and believe that the emotions we feel,
the words we speak, and the self we carry can reach others directly and accurately.
But the world rotates. We stand on a massive spinning disc.
So even though we exist in the same space, words miss their mark, expressions are misunderstood,
and love goes astray. My emotions and intentions draw a curve different from what I expect,
and my words bend before they reach you.

This work captures the subtle understanding and misunderstanding
that two people in love face, even while living in different worldviews.
It quietly asks if love can go beyond differences in values,
and if we truly look at the same world as the one we love.
Is the person we love really living in the same world as us?
